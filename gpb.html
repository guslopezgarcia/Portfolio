The Objection Battlecard

An objection is rarely the real reason for hesitation — it is the easiest thing to say. Your job is not to argue it away. Your job is to stay curious long enough for the real reason to surface.

SaaS Callout

How SaaS Buying Is Different

SaaS buyers are evaluating implementation risk, adoption, support quality, integrations, security, and whether the vendor can become a long-term operating partner.

Recurring revenue changes the conversation

  • Churn matters as much as acquisition.
  • Adoption determines renewal.
  • Customer Success is part of the value proposition.
  • A closed deal that never launches becomes future churn.

Speed and credibility matter

  • The first vendor to build a credible business case often shapes the evaluation.
  • Buyers expect integrations, security, and onboarding to be discussed early.
  • Data visibility is frequently the real product being purchased.

Part I

The Sales Philosophy

The thinking underneath every SaaS conversation: customers buy outcomes, not software. Your job is to translate product capabilities into measurable business impact.

Chapter 01 · Part I

How Buyers Actually Buy

Principle №1
Customers do not buy your product. They buy confidence that tomorrow’s operation will run better than today’s.

Every call, email, discovery question, and follow-up should reinforce one idea: you exist to improve their business, not to replace a tool.

BDR / Inside Sales callout

Listen for operational pain, not feature requests. When a prospect says “we need better reporting,” ask:

  • “What decision is hard to make today?”
  • “What happens when that decision is delayed?”
  • “Who feels the impact first?”

Your goal is to uncover the business consequence behind the request.

The real job to be done

Buyers rarely start evaluating SaaS because they enjoy shopping for software. Something has stopped scaling.

  • Pipeline has plateaued.
  • Reporting is unreliable.
  • Teams spend too much time in spreadsheets.
  • Customers are dropping during onboarding.
  • Managers lack visibility into performance.

SaaS metrics that matter

In SaaS, executives think in recurring revenue and retention, not just one-time sales.

MRR Monthly Recurring Revenue Short-term trend visibility
CAC Customer Acquisition Cost Acquisition efficiency
LTV Customer Lifetime Value Long-term profitability
Churn Customers or revenue lost Retention health
NRR Net Revenue Retention Expansion + retention performance
BDR / Inside Sales callout

Listen for retention pain. If a prospect mentions onboarding delays, low adoption, or poor visibility, that is not just a support problem — it is a future churn problem.

Chapter 08 · Part II

The SaaS Discovery Framework

Use this sequence in almost every SaaS discovery call
1

Current State

How is the process handled today? What tools, spreadsheets, or manual steps are involved?

How are you doing this today? Which systems are involved?
2

Pain

Where does the workflow break down? Look for delays, duplicate work, errors, and lack of visibility.

Where does it become painful? What is most frustrating for the team?
3

Impact

Translate the pain into time, revenue, headcount, customer experience, or forecasting impact.

What is that costing you? Who feels the impact first?
4

Future State

Define what success would look like six months after implementation.

What would improve first? How would you measure success?
5

Decision Process

Understand stakeholders, budget ownership, security review, procurement, and implementation timing.

Who else is involved? What does approval look like?
Inside Sales coaching

Don’t leave discovery without a quantified impact. Even a rough estimate is better than none: hours lost per week, revenue delayed, leads going untouched, or customers dropping during onboarding.

Glossary & Acronyms

Term Definition
API Application Programming Interface — allows systems to exchange data.
ARR Annual Recurring Revenue.
BDR Business Development Representative — focuses on outbound prospecting and qualification.
CAC Customer Acquisition Cost.
Churn Customers or revenue lost during a period.
CRM Customer Relationship Management system used to track accounts, contacts, and opportunities.
ICP Ideal Customer Profile — the type of company most likely to get value from your solution.
KPI Key Performance Indicator — a measurable business metric tied to success.
LTV / CLV Customer Lifetime Value — total expected revenue from a customer relationship.
MRR Monthly Recurring Revenue.
NRR Net Revenue Retention — retention plus expansion revenue from existing customers.
POC Proof of Concept — a limited validation that the solution can work in the customer’s environment.
ROI Return on Investment — financial return generated compared with the cost.
SE Sales Engineer — technical specialist who supports evaluations and integrations.
TTV Time-to-Value — how quickly a customer experiences meaningful value after purchase.
Win Rate Percentage of qualified opportunities that become closed-won deals.
